# Thumbnail psychology — the high-CTR set

Four editable templates (1280×720 SVG + PNG), each engineered around one click trigger.
Swap the **face** into the frame, change the **1–3 words / number**, export.

| File | Trigger | Why it works | Use it for |
|---|---|---|---|
| `thumb-A-fear` | **Fear / loss aversion** | We act harder to *avoid loss* than to gain. "STOP", red WARNING, "you're losing X". | "you're doing it wrong", mistakes, costly habits |
| `thumb-B-greed` | **Greed / gain** | A big concrete number + "FREE" + up-arrow promises reward fast. | money/results, "$X with AI", before→after |
| `thumb-C-curiosity` | **Curiosity (open loop)** | An unanswered question ("NOBODY TELLS YOU THIS?!") creates an itch only the click scratches. | secrets, "nobody tells you", reveals |
| `thumb-D-human` | **Human / emotion** | Faces grab the eye first; a strong expression + "INSANE." transfers emotion. | reactions, demos, big moments |

## The rules baked in (keep them when you edit)
1. **1–3 words, huge.** If you can't read it on a phone, it's too small.
2. **One emotional face**, big, looking at camera or the key element. (Drop yours in the frame.)
3. **High contrast** — near-black bg, one bright/gradient hero word. One **red** is reserved for fear/circles/arrows.
4. **Red circle + arrow** = "look here." Points the eye at the face or the payoff.
5. **A curiosity gap** — the thumbnail raises a question the title answers (don't give it all away).
6. **Numbers beat adjectives** — "$10,000" > "a lot of money"; "12 HRS/WK" > "tons of time".
7. **Thumbnail + title make the SAME promise.** Mismatch = bounce = the algorithm buries you.

## Workflow
- Drop your headshot into the dashed **frame** (in Canva/Figma/Photoshop after importing the SVG, or just layer the PNG behind your cut-out face).
- Edit the headline text in the SVG (it's plain `<text>`), re-export PNG at 1280×720.
- **A/B test 2–3** per video (YouTube's "Test & Compare", or swap after 24–48h). Fear vs. Greed vs. Curiosity usually separates fast.
- Keep faces **right or left**, text on the opposite side — never let text cover the eyes.

> Honest note: emotional ≠ misleading. Over-promise in the thumbnail and the watch-time
> drop tanks the video. Make the bold claim *true*.
